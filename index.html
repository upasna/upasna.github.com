Research

I have worked on three projects so far which are contiguous to my interest.

I started off with a project that aimed at generating Biogas from Water Hyacinth. The project was undertaken within a period of 60 days, June- August 2012. My guide and mentor through the course of events was J.H.Patil, Assistant Professor, RVCE, Bangalore.

This project followed a paper titled- ”Kinetics of Anaerobic Digestion of Water Hyacinth using Primary Sludge as Inoculum” which was presented at the- “International Conference on Green Technology and Environmental Conservation” organized by Satyabhama University in association with IEEE, Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board, ISWA (International Solid Waste Association)


The second project was part of my curriculum, here at RVCE, Bangalore. It dealt with “Development and Characterization of Composite Laminates”. The project was under the guidance of Dr. R. Suresh, HOD, Chemical Department, RVCE, Bangalore.
We developed glass laminates as well as natural laminates using jute and bamboo fibers. We also developed hybrid laminates such as glass/jute, glass/bamboo and jute/bamboo with vinyl and polyester resins.
Characterization included conducting tests such Water Absorption Test, Corrosion Test and Flame Test in accordance to the ASTM standards.


Finally, the third project titled- “Adsorption Studies of Porous Materials at Cryogenic Temperatures”, at Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore, was conducted under the guidance of Dr. Kasturirengan, Professor and Chairman, Centre for Cryogenic Technology (CCT), IISc, Bangalore.
